## 1. COVERAGE INCREASING Strait of Hormuz crisis
_Geopolitics & War / USA Politics / Economy — importance 3.9 — 13 sources — latest reported 2026-05-02 19:53 UTC_

**What changed today:** Today adds Trump’s formal claim to Congress that hostilities with Iran have ended, even as peace talks remain stalled, Iran warns Washington to choose diplomacy or war, and shipping and energy actors keep adapting to a mostly shut Hormuz.

President Donald Trump told congressional leaders in a Friday letter, reported late May 1 and followed up on May 2, that hostilities with Iran have terminated, a move that appears intended to avoid the War Powers Act’s 60-day deadline for congressional authorization. Democrats and several outlets frame the letter as an effort to bypass Congress, while the administration argues the early-April cease-fire ended or paused the legal clock. The claim sits uneasily beside continuing U.S. naval enforcement, sanctions threats against shippers paying Iranian tolls, and Trump’s own remarks that talks are not getting there.

Iran has sent a new peace proposal through Pakistani mediators, but Trump said he was not satisfied; Iranian officials warned that the U.S. must choose diplomacy or war and said Tehran is prepared for both. The Strait of Hormuz remains mostly shut, oil and jet fuel markets are strained, and carriers are adapting: MSC is avoiding Hormuz with a new Gulf service, Syria is emerging as an overland oil corridor, and airlines and travelers face higher costs.

Watch whether Congress challenges the War Powers maneuver, whether Iran formalizes control over Hormuz, and whether any tanker seizure or sanctions case turns the maritime standoff into a broader legal and military confrontation.

If no-go areas continue to expand, the practical geography of Gaza may become more fragmented, making recovery, aid delivery and any durable cease-fire harder to sustain.

For families already dealing with trauma, food insecurity and exposure, pests can make tents and temporary shelters feel unsafe even during moments of rest.

Watch whether aid agencies can restore waste collection, pest control, water systems and basic clinic access. Without environmental repair, medical treatment alone will not solve the problem; infestations will keep returning wherever people are forced to live amid rubble, garbage and inadequate shelter.
